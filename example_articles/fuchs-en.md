# The Red Fox - Clever Survivor

The red fox is the most widespread predator in the world. Its intelligence and adaptability have earned it a legendary reputation.

## Distribution

Red foxes are true cosmopolitans and live in:
- Europe
- Asia
- North America
- North Africa
- Australia (introduced)

They occupy almost every habitat—from forests and meadows to urban areas.

## Intelligence and adaptation

Foxes are known for their cleverness:
- Excellent memory for food caches
- Complex hunting strategies
- Rapid adaptation to new environments
- Successful urban animals in many metropolises

## Diet

As omnivores, foxes have a varied diet:
- **Main food**: mice and other small mammals
- Birds and their eggs
- Insects and earthworms
- Berries and fruit
- In cities: waste and compost

## Typical hunting behavior

The famous "mouse pounce" is typical of foxes. They leap onto their prey with precise movements and can hear and catch mice even under a snow cover of up to one meter.

## Social life

Foxes are generally territorial:
- Live mostly in pairs or small family groups
- Build complex dens with multiple exits
- Mark their territory with scent
- Communicate with various sounds

## Key facts

- **Weight**: 5-10 kg
- **Length**: 60-90 cm (without tail)
- **Tail length**: 30-50 cm
- **Speed**: Up to 50 km/h
- **Life expectancy**: 2-5 years in the wild

## Cultural significance

In fables and stories worldwide, the fox is portrayed as a symbol of cunning and trickery. From Reynard the Fox to the Japanese kitsune, the fox has fascinated people for millennia.
