# The African Elephant

The African elephant is the largest land mammal on Earth. With its distinctive trunk and impressive tusks, it is a symbol of the African wilderness.

## Habitat

African elephants live in the savannas, forests, and deserts south of the Sahara. They need large areas to roam and can travel up to 50 kilometers per day.

## Diet

An adult elephant can consume up to 150 kg of plant material per day. Their diet consists of:
- Grasses
- Leaves
- Bark
- Fruits
- Roots

## Social behavior

Elephants live in matriarchal herds led by the oldest and most experienced cow. The social bonds within the herd are very strong.

## Threats and protection

Unfortunately, African elephants are threatened by poaching and habitat loss. Their ivory makes them a target for illegal hunters. International conservation programs are working to preserve these majestic animals.

## Key facts

- **Weight**: Up to 6 tons
- **Height**: Up to 4 meters at the shoulder
- **Life expectancy**: 60-70 years
- **Gestation**: 22 months (longest of all land animals)
